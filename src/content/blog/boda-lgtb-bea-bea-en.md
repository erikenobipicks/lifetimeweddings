## An LGBTQ wedding in Tarragona

![Bea and Bea at their intimate LGBTQ wedding in Castellvell del Camp](/blog/boda-lgtb-bea-bea/2d4f94_c9c0c27cc03245ffae79c2e5396b6222~mv2.jpg)

Hi everyone! Today I want to introduce you to [Bea](https://www.instagram.com/peterpan_29/) and [Bea](https://www.instagram.com/beatriz_lofer/), two women who got married on March 7th, 2020 in [Castellvell del Camp](https://www.google.com/maps/place/Castellvell+del+Camp,+Tarragona/data=!4m2!3m1!1s0x12a15176690fb551:0x3ed43d0180463d68). It was an **intimate LGBTQ wedding**, attended by their **closest family and friends**.

[Bea](https://www.instagram.com/peterpan_29/)'s mum came to visit us for a small family session on the wedding day at our studio — she arrived just three days before. Luckily we had the date free, since the busy wedding season hadn't kicked off yet.

The idea of doing the family studio photos on any given day of the year made sense. My proposal, since this was an intimate LGBTQ wedding, was to come straight to the ceremony and shoot the group photos Bea's mum wanted after that.

That way they could also have more artistic photos of the couple, because their original idea was that guests would take pictures with phones that day. But my reply was:

*Would they know how to tell a story? Could they catch the beautiful moments and make you feel something when you look at them later?*

My job is to **tell stories** with my photos, **catching moments** that make the couple and their family **get emotional looking back** at them years later. I guess they understood we go a little beyond just a few photos, that we try to tell that story, that spark between the couple, to catch the emotions — among many other moments. In the end they decided to book us.

## The LGBTQ wedding

As I mentioned, it was an intimate wedding. [Bea](https://www.instagram.com/beatriz_lofer/) walked in singing a song that moved [Bea](https://www.instagram.com/peterpan_29/), her bride-to-be, to tears. Another detail that caught my eye: [Bea](https://www.instagram.com/peterpan_29/)'s grandmother was the one who brought the rings to the brides — something I hadn't seen at any of my previous weddings, and it felt like a beautiful, meaningful touch.
